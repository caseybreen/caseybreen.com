Overview

The growing availability of new streams of data, expanding computational power, and the digitalization of social life have created new questions and research opportunities for social and population scientists. This course introduces students to core methodological and substantive topics in computational social science.

Topics include digital trace data, machine learning, non-probability sampling, social networks, and agent-based modeling. The course combines seminar-style discussion with hands-on lab sessions in R, emphasizing engagement with existing research and practical application of computational methods.
